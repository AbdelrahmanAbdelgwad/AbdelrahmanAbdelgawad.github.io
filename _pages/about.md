I am a second-year PhD student in [Systems Engineering](https://www.bu.edu/systems/) at Boston University, advised by [Prof. Roberto Tron](https://sites.bu.edu/tron/) and [Prof. Sean B. Andersson](https://sites.bu.edu/andersson/). I am a member of the [BU Robotics Lab](https://sites.bu.edu/robotics/) and I also collaborate closely with [Prof. Renato Mancuso](https://rmancuso.faculty.cs.bu.edu/) at the CPS Lab.

My research focuses on making **neural network controllers practically certifiable** for safety-critical robotic systems. I develop methods at the intersection of control theory, machine learning, and formal verification — with the goal of enabling resource-constrained robots to learn and execute complex behaviors while providing formal stability and safety guarantees.

Specifically, my work addresses the fundamental computational bottleneck in neural Lyapunov methods, where traditional approaches require solving NP-hard Mixed Integer Programs. I develop specification-driven approaches that dramatically reduce the cost of formal verification while maintaining provable guarantees.

My research is supported by [NSF FRR-2212051](https://www.nsf.gov/awardsearch/showAward?AWD_ID=2212051).
